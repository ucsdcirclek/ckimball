What is Masquerade Ball?

La Jolla Kiwanis and UC San Diego Circle K cordially invites you to the 22nd Annual Masquerade Ball, the most extravagant night of the season. Join us as we Uncover Lost Riches at our first ever large-scale Kiwanis Family event and fundraiser! Masquerade Ball, originally hosted by UCSD Circle K, is an annual fundraiser that supports one of CNH Circle K's District Fundraising Initiatives (DFIs).

This year's event will feature an opportunity for Kiwanis Family members to intermingle and connect, all while raising money for the Trevor Project. The event itself will feature a three-course meal, a silent disco, ballroom dance lessons, an escape room, and more!

NOTE: This event is only for Kiwanis members, Circle K International members, and approved guests.

Our Cause: The Trevor Project

The Trevor Project is a national non-profit organization dedicated toward suicide prevention for LGBTQ youth. The Trevor Project works to provide support to individuals in crisis through counseling services.

Their mission is to create a safe, inclusive space and sense of community among LGBTQ youth to reduce risk and end suicide.

They offer a wide supportive resources ranging from a 24/7 crisis hotline to workshops and training in prevention. In addition to these resources, they support the LGBTQ community through advocacy for further preventative education in schools.

Alcohol Policy

There is a no alcohol tolerance policy for Circle K members during Masquerade Ball 2019. Regardless of age, Circle K members and their guests will not be permitted access to wristbands for the bar and there will be security checking ID's and wristbands. If wristbands are torn or tampered in any way, those individuals will be turned away and have their wristbands confiscated. Security has full authority to question and/or remove any guests who fail to comply to these regulations.
